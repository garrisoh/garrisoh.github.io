Prior Work Experience

Metso Minerals

During the summer of 2012, I worked full-time as an intern at Metso Minerals in York, PA. There I assisted one of the electrical engineers in the creation and revision of several different types of electrical diagrams, including:

  • Process & Instrumentation Diagram
  • Instrumentation I/O List
  • Recommended Logic Schematic
  • Functional Description (Control Philosophy)
  • Motor Load List
I created these diagrams for two different types of mineral grinding mills: the VERTIMILL® and the Stirred Media Detritor (SMD) The electrical diagrams for these machines focused on the instrumentation and control systems necessary to operate the mills. I created and revised these diagrams using programs such as AutoCAD and Microsoft Excel and learned how to use the SAP database system to store documents and track revisions.

While at Metso, I was also able to experience a professional environment. I learned how to conduct myself in such a situation, and I was also exposed to the engineering process. I was able to sit in on several meetings and observe discussions on projects at various stages of development. I also had the opportunity to see parts of the machines I was working on in the field, allowing me to see beyond the design stages and into the construction stage. Overall, I believe that it was a fantastic first experience for a first technical internship.

International Experience

As part of an immersion requirement for my International Studies degree, I went on a study abroad trip to Madrid, Spain in the spring semester of 2013. This trip is hosted each year by the engineering program at Lafayette. A group of 25 students and a Lafayette professor go abroad for a semester and study at Saint Louis University (SLU), Madrid. I lived with a Spanish host family in the city and had a 20 minute walk to school each day. This was a fantastic experience as I got to experience the Spanish culture first-hand and practice speaking Spanish with my host parents every evening at the dinner table.

While I was abroad, I spent breaks traveling around Spain and Europe. We took class trips to Segovia, Toledo, El Escorial, and San Sebastián in Spain as well as to Toulouse, France and Morocco. I also visited Trujillo, Cáceres, and Mérida in Spain, Brussels, Belgium, and visited a friend studying abroad in Dresden, Germany. Volunteerism

While I was abroad, I also took advantage of a volunteer opportunity that I found at SLU, Madrid. I began volunteering at one of the "colegio's" (similar to elementary school) by helping the students with their English homework in an after-school study session. I would spend about an hour every Monday and Wednesday with the kids (ages from around 9-12) helping with homework assignments, preparing for exams, and reviewing vocabulary. We often played pictionary or hangman in English to practice vocabulary and spelling. It was a challenging, but very rewarding experience for me to be able to help the kids and provide them with the opportunity to hear a native speaker.
