Motion Control

The Motion Control project began in the beginning of my sophomore year when a friend and I decided we wanted to explore 3D motion sensing technology. We found a project idea online and received funding from the Lafayette College ECE department. The project was to build our own 3D motion sensor. It consisted of three square pieces of cardboard with aluminum foil glued to one side of each piece. The three pieces were taped together at one corner to form half of a cube. Then using an Arduino microcontroller board, we applied a charge to each of the three plates and measured the voltage at each one. These plates could then act as variable capacitances as we moved our hand around inside the cube. By measuring the voltage at each plate, we can then calculate the position of our hand. We used this sensor to control the mouse on a computer screen where moving our hand forward signaled a mouse click.

After completing this project, we decided to continue exploring motion control but to increase the scale and difficulty of the project. We merged with our school's American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) club as one of the long-term projects or "tech teams." These teams function as smaller clubs within ASME to work on projects related to a specific area of study.

Our new project concept was to control a robotic arm with a LeapMotion controller. The robot arm that we used belonged to the ECE department for use with our Digital Circuits II course. We assembled a team of students with me as the team leader and began working on this new project. After just over a semester of working on this project, we completed our goal. The video above shows the project in action, displaying basic movement, grip action controlled by the openness of our hand, and my (highly uncoordinated) attempt at stacking blocks.

For the remainder of the semester and in future semesters, we plan to explore the applications of the LeapMotion controller to flight control by using the sensor to control a quadcopter. As this project progresses, I will further update this page with information on the new project.

The source code for the project can be found on Github. The project was written in Java. For examples of my code, you can look at SerialManager.java, SerialComm.java, View.java, and RobotArduino.ino (other classes were written by other team members).
