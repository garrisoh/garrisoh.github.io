About Me

I was essentially born and raised in Pennsylvania. I lived briefly in Baton Rouge, Louisianna for a few years when I was very little. My younger sister, Beth, was born there, and when I was four, my family moved to Southern York County, PA. The snowy image shown above is a view looking out the window of the house where I grew up. To me, it is one of the most beautiful places in the world, and I enjoy spending my summers and school breaks there when I can. Both of my parents work nearby as Mechanical Engineering professors at York College of Pennsylvania, and my sister is currently a freshman at Muhlenberg College.


My cat, Nala, but everyone calls her Jeanie.

I have always been a big animal lover. At home, I have a cat, Nala, that I adore and two dogs, T-bone and Elmo. At the age of four, I began to learn how to ride horses. I became an active member of the United States Pony Clubs and frequently attended competitions in a wide variety of horse disciplines, including hunt seat, show jumping, dressage, eventing, and mounted games. I also enjoyed training my horses. When I was about twelve years old, I rescued a small pony from an abusive situation. I taught him to trust people and broke him to ride and drive a cart. I continued working with horses up until college, when I found that I no longer had the time to give to the sport.

Growing up, I attended Southern Elementary and Middle Schools and Susquehannock High school from grades K-12. My senior year at Susquehannock, I did dual enrollment at York College, so I spent mornings taking several courses at York and returned to high school in the afternoon. In spring of that year, I ended up taking Engineering Physics with "professor Dad." Good thing I did well in that course... In 2011, I graduated from Susquehannock as the Salutatorian of my class.

Currently, I am a junior ECE and International Studies dual major at Lafayette College. I have had a great experience at Lafayette, and it has become like a second home to me. I live in a college-owned off-campus house that is part of a group of Living Learning Communities (LLC's). Each house is given a name related to a specific topic, and students in each house are expected to hold events related to the topic. We are the technology house, so we hold events related to hands-on exploration of modern and ancient technology. We have done everything from chainmaille to textiles to building solar chargers. I love the sense of community that living in this house has brought me. We have organized a cooking co-op, so each of us cooks one night of the week and we all get to chat while we eat homemade dinner every night. It has been a great way to meet new people and to relax after a long day of classes.

In the future, I would like to go to graduate school. I would love to be able to study robotics (because let's be honest, robots are awesome). I have worked on several projects involving robots (see the projects link above), and I have enjoyed every minute of them. I find myself especially drawn to flying robots and would love to do more projects with them.
