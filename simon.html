Simon


The classic Simon game

Last semester, as part of my software engineering course, we were tasked with designing a game for one of the psychology professors at Lafayette College. This game is based on the classic Simon game where users must follow the ever increasing sequence of lights until they make a mistake. The professor plans to use this game to study the effects of different variables on a person's working memory. The game allows the professor to turn on and off sound, color, and different input devices. We were required to use three different types of input devices: the keyboard, the mouse, and a LeapMotion controller. All user trial data is written out to a CSV file, including participant demographics, timing, trial parameters, and the longest sequence of lights pressed.
